Research

Current Research Interests

  • [Cryptography and Number Theory] I'm interested in the mathematical foundations of encryption systems, with particular interest in RSA and its vulnerability to classical algorithms.
  • [Optimization and Game Theory] I'm also interested in large-scale optimization problems involving risk, especially in the context of operations research and financial systems.
  • [Algebraic Structures and Representation Theory] I like studying Lie groups and their representations, with a focus on automorphic forms, to understand symmetry in number-theoretic and geometric contexts.

Projects

  • MATH 6230: Optimal Surveillance Strategy in Stackelberg Games
    Fall 2024 | Cornell University
    Developed a mathematical model for evader-observer dynamics using Stackelberg security game theory. Designed a large-scale linear program to simulate adversarial path-planning in grid environments. Iteratively optimized surveillance distributions based on evader responses to maximize detection probability under resource constraints.

    Tools: Python (Gurobi, Matplotlib, NumPy, PuLP)
    Impact: Offers applications in military surveillance.

  • NSF-REU: Zeros of Period Polynomials
    Summer 2024 | University of Michigan-Dearborn
    Studied zero distributions in period polynomials related to a variant of the Riemann Hypothesis. Used Mathematica to visualize and analyze zeros over imaginary quadratic fields and complex domains. Presented findings at mathematics conferences.

    Tools: Mathematica, Magma, LMFDB
    Impact: Contributes to ongoing research on L-functions and modular forms.

  • MATH 7390: Representation Theory and Automorphic Forms
    Spring 2024 | Cornell University
    Explored deep connections between the representation theory of SL(2,R) and automorphic forms on the upper half-plane. Investigated the structure of Lie groups via Casimir operators and bi-invariant differential operators and discussed the Iwasawa and Bruhat decompositions. Here is the project.
  • MATH 4901: Directed Reading Program on L-Functions
    Spring 2024 | Cornell University
    Completed an advanced Directed Reading Program on L-functions. Studied analytic properties of L-functions and modular forms, culminating in an original presentation to faculty and graduate mentors.
  • ORIE 4999: Polytopes
    Fall 2023 | Cornell University
    Conducted literature review on Polynomial Hirsch Conjecture and Barnette’s Conjecture. Analyzed the combinatorial and geometric properties of polytopes and convex hulls, with attention to counterexamples and their implications in linear programming.

    Outcome: Assesses progress toward resolving two major open problems in discrete geometry and optimization theory.

  • NSF-REU: Numerical Methods for Optical Design
    Summer 2023 | University of Rochester
    Modeled freeform optical surfaces using orthogonal polynomial interpolation (Legendre and Chebyshev bases). Investigated convergence behavior and surface-fitting precision for lens geometries. Presented findings to faculty and peers.

    Tools: MATLAB
    Impact: Supports design of high-precision optical systems in imaging and photonics.

  • ORIE 4999: Optimizing University Course Scheduling
    Spring 2023 | Cornell University
    Led a team to develop a large-scale linear program for optimizing university-wide course scheduling. Integrated course, instructor, room, and other constraints using real administrative datasets.

    Tools: Python, Excel
    Impact: Solution is currently used by the Registrar for scheduling; findings presented at the Cornell Undergraduate Research Symposium.
